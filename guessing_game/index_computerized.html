Battleship!

Unfortunately, this study does not work in your current browser. Please use Chrome, Firefox, or Safari. Sorry!

Here, you'll play a randomness game against an opponent.

Specifically, you'll be playing a game that's a lot like battleship. Importantly, you'll be playing against an opponent who has previously placed their ship (and is not doing it live).

Instead of playing on one big board that remains across turns (like in normal battleship), you'll play on a new mini-board each turn.

Here's how it will work:

Your opponent has placed a ship in a random tile on each trial. They don't want you to guess its location, so they are trying to be unpredictable. They generated these sequences previously, so you're just playing against the choices they previously made. (For that reason, the game will go fast!)

You will be trying to guess the random tile, thus sinking your opponent's ship. Remember that your opponent previously placed their ships in a way that was unpredictable.

The game will last 150 turns in total.

To continue, press the → key on your keyboard.

There will also be a point system in the game, just to make things more engaging. Every time you computer can't get your opponent's ship, they'll get +1. On the other hand, if you do guess it correctly, then you'll get +4 if you guess it on the first try, +3 if you guess it on the second try, and +2 if you guess it on the third try.
